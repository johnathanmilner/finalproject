Low hospitalization rates at nursing homes is associated with higher quality care, as it means that resident care needs are met at the facility without the need to disrupt the resident. Medicare and Medicaid funded Nursing homes in the United States report regularly the number of hospitalizations per 1,000 long-stay resident days. This data, along with other measures, is available publicly in the U.S. Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services website.


Quality improvement requires not only knowing what the current rate is, but understanding what is influencing the rate. Understanding what is affecting the rate can lead to insights of potential actions that can be done to improve the rate.


For this project, I collected data from four different U.S. Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services data sets, analyzed which factors were correlated with hospitalization rates, and developed a machine learning model to explain how the factors affect the hospitalization rate at 11,852 nursing homes in the United States.
